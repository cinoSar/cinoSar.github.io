About This Resource

This course explores the economics of environmental problems — why markets fail in the presence of pollution and public goods, and how policy instruments can restore efficiency. It is designed as a companion to the tutorial sessions for the Environmental Economics and Resource Valuation module at the University of Padua (Dept. ICEA).

How We Teach

Each lesson follows a proven learning sequence:

  1. Real-World Problem: We start with a concrete environmental or resource problem that motivates the economic concept you will learn.
  2. Intuition Building: We develop your intuition through clear explanations and relatable examples — before any algebra.
  3. Graphical Solutions: We visualise the problem geometrically, so you can see the relationships between costs, benefits, and welfare.
  4. Mathematical Formalisation: Finally, we formalise the concepts for precision and rigour, building towards the exercises you will tackle in tutorials.

This progression from concrete to abstract ensures you understand not just the mechanics but the economic reasoning behind environmental policy.

Course Structure

The course is organised in three progressive blocks:

  1. Foundations: Competitive market review, public goods and free-riding — the baseline you need before analysing market failures.
  2. Market Failures and Welfare: Externalities, the private versus social optimum, welfare analysis, and the tools for measuring inefficiency.
  3. Policy Instruments: Pigouvian taxation, tradeable emission permits, the Coase theorem, and how to choose between these approaches when information is imperfect.
