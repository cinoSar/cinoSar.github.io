About

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Padua, working on the territorial impacts of logistics infrastructure. My research investigates the trade-offs between national logistics efficiency (TEN-T corridors) and local negative externalities — congestion, land use, environmental costs — generated by freight villages. I combine spatial analysis with multi-criteria evaluation methods to study these socio-economic impacts.

Previously, I worked as a research fellow on European projects related to energy communities and cost-benefit analysis of environmental interventions. I hold a Master's degree in Economics and Finance from the University of Padua and a Bachelor's degree in Foreign Trade from Ca' Foscari University of Venice.

Teaching

I serve as a teaching assistant (tutorial sessions) at the University of Padua, preparing and delivering exercise classes that complement the main lectures. My sessions focus on guided problem-solving, working through exercises step by step so that students build both analytical technique and economic intuition.

Environmental Economics and Resource Valuation

A.Y. 2024–2025 · Dept. ICEA · Prof. C. D'Alpaos · English

Tutorial sessions covering the microeconomic foundations of environmental policy: public goods and free-riding, externalities and the social optimum, Pigouvian taxation, the Coase theorem, and tradeable emission permits. Exercises progress from analytical derivations to numerical welfare comparisons.

📐

Fondamenti di Economia e Valutazione dei Progetti

A.Y. 2025–2026 · Dept. ICEA · Prof. R. Canesi & C. D'Alpaos · Italian

Tutorial sessions covering the microeconomics and financial mathematics modules of the course. Microeconomics topics include consumer theory (utility maximisation with Cobb-Douglas, perfect substitutes, and Leontief preferences), demand derivation from first principles, horizontal aggregation of individual demands, and monetary welfare measures (compensating and equivalent variation). Financial mathematics topics include interest calculations, annuities, and amortisation schedules.

Research

My current PhD project, supervised by Prof. Chiara D'Alpaos and Prof. Luciano Greco, examines the territorial paradox of logistics hubs — how infrastructure that benefits national supply chains can simultaneously impose costs on local communities. I am also working on a systematic literature review on the spatial impacts of logistics hubs.
